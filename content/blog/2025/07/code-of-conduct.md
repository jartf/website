This is my personal code of conduct. Nobody has to agree with it, but it's the way I live, and it's what I expect people to keep in mind if they want to deal with me respectfully.

I don't perform for anyone. If I say something, I mean it. If I do something, I stand by it. I'd rather deal with uncomfortable truth than hide behind empty politeness. I'll meet people halfway if there's mutual respect, but never at the cost of my own integrity. My values aren't for sale.

Respect is non-negotiable, and it has to go both ways. If you give it, you'll get it. If you don't, don't expect me to waste time on playing nice or polite neutrality. I'm not interested in smiling through dishonesty, cruelty, or superiority. You either come at me with respect and sincerity, or you get distance. If you tell me something matters to you, I'll take it seriously. I expect the same back. Break that trust, and you won't get another chance.

I value depth and curiosity. I want to be around people who are open-minded. I don't care for shallow connections or people who pretend to know everything. If you carry yourself with fake superiority, we won't click. What I respect are people willing to learn, willing to question, and willing to listen.

I don't chase people. If you want to be in my life, you'll show up. If you don't, the door is always open both ways. I'm fine on my own.

Humor isn't my strong suit. Some people think I don't know how to joke, and that's fine. I'd rather be understood than just treated like a source of entertainment. But if you can actually make me laugh, or laugh with me, I'll value that more than you think.

Toxicity has no place in my life. I cut it out without hesitation, and I don't care even if you're family, friend, or stranger. If you're not a good influence in my life, you don't get to stay. My time and energy are limited, and I protect them like they're gold. I live by observation and reflection. I don't just react blindly, I watch, I think, I reflect, I adapt. I take responsibility for my actions. If I screw up, I'll own it. If I succeed, I'll try not to let it go to my head.

At the end of the day, my code is simple: be honest, respect boundaries, cut the crap, stay curious, and move forward.
